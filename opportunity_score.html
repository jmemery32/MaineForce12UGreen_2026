What This Measures

Weighted innings played by position across the full season. Each position carries a weight reflecting how much active development it provides. A pitcher is in every play; a left fielder may not touch the ball for an inning. The score captures how much meaningful fielding development time each player received.

Position Weights

P3.0Every pitch, highest development
C2.5Constant engagement, field general
SS2.0Most demanding infield spot
2B / 3B / CF1.5Active infield / premier outfield
1B1.2Infield, more passive
LF / RF1.0Baseline outfield

Raw vs. Rate

Raw Score = Σ (innings × weight). Reflects absolute development time.

Opportunity Rate = Raw ÷ (GP × 6). Normalizes for players who missed games — the fairer comparison.

Coaching Goal

A well-distributed team sees most players between 0.9–1.3 on Opp Rate. Outliers below 0.8 may signal a kid who needs more high-value innings next season. Outliers above 1.4 reflect heavy investment in premium spots (pitching/SS/C).

What This Measures

Batting order position determines how many plate appearances a player gets over a game. A leadoff hitter bats roughly 20–25% more often than the cleanup hitter in a short game, and the bottom of the order even less. This score captures how much offensive opportunity the coach gave each player through lineup placement.

Formula

For each game a player appeared in, their lineup spot is normalized to a 0–1 scale:

Spot Score = (Roster Size − Lineup Pos) ÷ (Roster Size − 1)

1.0 = leadoff. 0.0 = last in order. Average across all games = Offensive Opportunity Score.

Data Source

Batting orders drawn from all game plan HTML files: Apr 26 (G1/G2), May 3 (G1/G2), May 10 (G1/G2), May 17 (G1/G2), May 23 pool play (G1/G2), May 23 bracket (G1/G2/G3), and tournament games.

Coaching Goal

In a pure rotation approach everyone averages 0.50. Scores above 0.60 reflect consistent top-of-order placement; below 0.35 reflects bottom-of-order. Intentional — coaches put stronger hitters up top — but worth reviewing for players who deserve more run-scoring chances next year.

Combined Score

Blends Defensive Opportunity Rate and Offensive Opportunity Score using equal weight (50/50). Both scores are already normalized to comparable scales. The combined view shows which players received holistic opportunity — both in the field at premium spots and in the lineup at productive positions.

How to Read It

Above 0.90 = high opportunity across both dimensions.

0.65–0.90 = solid, well-rounded opportunity.

Below 0.65 = coach may want to revisit this player's role heading into next season.

What This Measures

How closely the actual season fielding statistics matched what was drawn up in the game plan HTML files. For each player, the planned proportion of innings at each position is compared to the actual proportion. Overlap in those distributions = adherence.

Method

For each position: compute the fraction of planned innings vs. actual innings. The adherence score is the sum of min(planned%, actual%) across all 9 positions — essentially the "intersection" of the two distributions. 100% = executed exactly as drawn up.

What Lowers It

In-game adjustments, injured arms, absent players, and defensive substitutions all cause deviations. A lower score doesn't mean bad coaching — it means the game demanded flexibility. Planners never survive contact with the opponent.

Season Summary

Overall weighted adherence across all 13 players: 84%. That's a meaningful number — it means roughly 5 out of 6 fielding innings went exactly as planned.
